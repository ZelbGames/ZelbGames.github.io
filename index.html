Projects

  • BONUS: Hide and Seek Society

    I was fortunate enough to inherit the UOB Hide and Seek society during my final year.

    During the first semester me and a carefully selected group of friends built and founded what was a name and an approval into a society proper.

    Resulting from these efforts UOB H&S Society currently puts in small to large scale games of hide and seek for between 5~50 players, bringing together a cross campus community.

  • World Driver

    World Driver is a technical demo written in C++ and Gdscript using the Godot engine.

    The game simulates all (available) real world roads on a 1:1 Scale using OSM data

    This offers the player unparalled access and freedom to explore and experience the world's roads

  • Wizards Versus Ninjas

    Wizards versus Ninjas was an ambitious VR RPG that leveraged the power of unreal engine and blueprints.

    The game executed a concept for a kinaesthetic input system where the various positions and orientations of the fingers and hands are used to control the inputs.

  • Wizard On A Skateboard

    Wizard On A Skateboard Is a classic 2D level based platformer. Gameplay is quick and focused with sharp controls to compliment the experience.

    The project was part of a range of smaller games I made to improve my overall development skills. I used each development cycle to apply conceptual knowledge I obtained from books on game design and experience learning C++ programming.

  • Pathfinder 2E Combat Manager (PF2E-CM)

    PF2E-CM is a character and initiative management tool for the PF2E TTRPG intended to reduce a DM's cognitive load.

    By evaluating rule-based outcomes for the DM, rulings are now presented as a list of options, intended to encourage creativity and dynamic game sessions.

  • Door To Door

    Door to Door is an ambitious game jam concept made in the unreal engine using bueprints

    Gameplay is a hybrid of elements from many genres and primarily consists of playing mini games to increase score via the trade for doors of higher value. But be cautious... trading doors is highly illegal and peril waits around every corner!

About Me

Profile

See picture right. (L-R) Toe Jammer. My Singing Monsters (video game), Sam Zakers. Game Developer – March 2023

My love for gameplay programming is rooted in an innate impulse to tinker. Later on, once I appreciated my drive to create entertaining experiences for others... I found that I wanted to make video games!

I studied Physics at the University of Birmingham, and I chose this path as I have always been curious about the ‘whys’, behind every ‘how’. This gave me a strong set of analytical skills... from mapping Chaos in the Solar System to constructing geometric spaces that allow us to comprehend the world of Quantum Mechanics. The degree gave me a set of underlying axioms that underpin a unique approach to developing solutions...all supported by the fundamental knowledge of advanced maths and physics.

Since graduating, I work developing games, across the extent of the development pipeline. I specialise in programming, focusing on gameplay elements. I have a passion for constructing experiences I can share with others.

As a programmer I write code that is concise, precise and easy to comprehend. My goal is code that represents the simplest possible invariant and stable solution, meaning that my code does only its intended job.

I enjoy working with people and at places that encourage and foster creativity, collaboration and teamwork.

A bit more

There are so many great games to play ... too many for any lifetime! I started gaming young, always looking for something unique, exciting or just interesting - I tend towards playing lots of different genres. I enjoy the act of experiencing a game and exploring what each offer, particularly sandbox, simulation and adventure games. It’s about exploration coupled with the freedom to influence your own experience.
